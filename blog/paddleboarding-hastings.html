Paddleboarding in Hastings: A Beginner's Guide to Coastal SUP

Fancy paddling along one of the South East's most iconic coastlines? Hastings offers a brilliant setting for paddleboarding, whether you're keen to try stand-up paddleboarding for the first time or you want to build your skills on the water. The combination of sheltered spots and open sea gives you options for every level, and our expert instructors will make sure you leave with a big grin and a huge sense of achievement.

Why Paddleboard in Hastings?

Hastings is a fantastic destination for paddleboarders of all levels. The town sits right on the Sussex coast, giving you access to varied paddling conditions. You've got calm, sheltered spots perfect for absolute beginners learning the basics, and more interesting coastal paddling if you're looking for a bit more challenge.

The local scenery is stunning. You'll paddle with views of the historic seafront, dramatic cliffs, and beautiful coastal landscapes. It's not just a workout, it's an experience. Plus, Hastings is home to award-winning watersports instruction, with qualified guides who know these waters inside out.

Another brilliant thing about Hastings paddleboarding is accessibility. The beach launches are straightforward, the facilities are good, and you don't need any previous experience. Whether you're coming solo, with family, or as a group of friends, you'll find a session that suits you.

Where to Paddleboard in Hastings

Hastings Beach

The main beach at Hastings is the most accessible spot for paddleboarding sessions. The gently shelving beach makes launching straightforward, and there are changing facilities and parking nearby. You can paddleboard along the seafront, enjoying the Victorian architecture and bustling beach atmosphere.

East Hastings and the Coastal Path

If you want to venture further, you can paddle east along the coast towards Warrior Square and beyond. This stretch offers quieter conditions, with beautiful coastal scenery and interesting coves to explore. It's perfect once you've got a bit of confidence under your belt.

Rock and Pool

Heading towards Filey, there are more protected areas and rockpools to explore. These spots are brilliant for intermediate paddleboarders who want calmer conditions but still want adventure.

What to Expect on Your First Session

Your first paddleboarding session in Hastings will start with a thorough safety briefing and equipment check. Our instructors will make sure your buoyancy aid is properly fitted, explain the basics of paddling technique, and answer any questions you might have.

You'll spend time getting comfortable on the board in shallow water before venturing further out. The instructors will teach you the fundamentals: how to hold the paddle, how to move from kneeling to standing, and how to maintain balance. Don't worry if it feels wobbly at first, everyone feels that way, and it clicks faster than you'd think.

Most beginners' sessions last 1.5 to 2 hours. You'll get a mix of instruction, guided practice, and time to explore at your own pace with an instructor nearby. The whole experience is designed to build confidence, so you're never pushed beyond what feels comfortable.

What to Wear and Paddleboarding in Hastings

Hastings is on the coast, so water temperature and weather conditions matter. In winter months, the sea is cold, and you'll definitely want a wetsuit. We provide wetsuits as part of your session, so you don't need to buy one.

In summer, you might paddleboard in just a rash guard or light top, especially if conditions are warm. Spring and autumn tend to need wetsuits, depending on how you feel about cold water.

Always bring: - Sunscreen (you'll be on the water and reflection off the water intensifies the sun) - A towel - A change of clothes - Water and snacks - Any medication you might need

Footwear is tricky on the beach, so wear something you're happy to get wet, or go barefoot. Neoprene shoes are brilliant if you've got sensitive feet.

Is Paddleboarding Hard? What About Safety?

Paddleboarding looks harder than it actually is. The board is more stable than you'd expect, and with proper instruction, most people are standing and paddling within the first session. The mental side is often bigger than the physical side, but our instructors are experts at building confidence.

Safety is paramount. All our sessions include a thorough briefing on water awareness, what to do if you fall (spoiler: it's fine, you just get back on), and how to stay safe in different conditions. You'll always wear a buoyancy aid, and instructors stay close by, especially for beginners.

Paddleboarding in Hastings vs Other Sussex Locations

Hastings is brilliant for coastal paddleboarding, but you might also want to explore other spots. The River Rother at Bodiam offers calmer, river-based paddling with stunning castle views. Paddleboarding in Sussex's rivers gives you a different feel from coastal paddling, with narrower waterways and more sheltered conditions.

The choice depends on what appeals to you. Coastal paddling gives you more space, interesting scenery, and a sense of adventure. River paddling is calmer and often more intimate, with beautiful countryside views.

Types of Paddleboarding Sessions in Hastings

Taster Sessions

Our shortest option, usually 1 hour, perfect for absolute beginners who want to try paddleboarding without a big time commitment. You'll learn the basics and decide if you want to pursue it further.

Standard Beginner Sessions

2 hours of instruction and guided paddling. Most people find this is the sweet spot for their first time, giving plenty of time to build confidence.

Social Paddles

Once you've got a bit of experience, join other paddleboarders for a relaxed paddle along the coast. These are brilliant for meeting people, building community, and enjoying paddling without a structured lesson.

SUP Yoga

If you fancy combining paddleboarding with wellness, our SUP yoga sessions are fantastic. You'll perform gentle yoga poses on the board in calm water, combining balance practice with relaxation.
