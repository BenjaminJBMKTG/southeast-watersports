Best Outdoor Activities in Bodiam, Kent: Water Sports & Local Adventures

Bodiam is one of those special places in Kent that many people drive past without realising what they're missing. This small village near the Kent-Sussex border offers some of the best outdoor activities in the region, from paddleboarding on historic waterways to exploring one of England's most iconic castles.

If you're looking for things to do in Bodiam, you're in for a treat. This isn't just about visiting a castle (though that's brilliant too). Bodiam sits in the heart of some of Kent's most beautiful countryside, with the River Rother winding through spectacular scenery and outdoor adventures waiting around every corner.

Let's explore exactly what makes Bodiam such a fantastic destination for outdoor enthusiasts and families looking for proper adventures.

Bodiam Castle: The Iconic Centrepiece

You can't talk about Bodiam without mentioning the castle. It's one of the most photographed castles in England, and when you see it, you'll understand why.

Built in 1385, Bodiam Castle sits perfectly in the middle of its square moat, looking exactly like a castle from a storybook. The reflection in the water, the battlements, the towers... it's genuinely stunning.

Visiting the Castle

The castle is managed by the National Trust and open most of the year. You can explore the interior, climb the towers for spectacular views across the Rother valley, and imagine what life was like for medieval knights and nobles.

Children absolutely love it. The spiral staircases, the battlements to explore, the stories of sieges and defences... it brings history alive in a way that books never quite manage.

Allow at least an hour to explore properly, longer if you want to enjoy the grounds and have a picnic by the moat. The setting is beautiful, and on a sunny day, it's hard to think of a nicer spot for lunch.

Water Sports on the River Rother

Here's where Bodiam gets really exciting. The River Rother here is perfect for watersports, particularly paddleboarding and kayaking.

Paddleboarding with a Castle Backdrop

Paddleboarding on the River Rother near Bodiam is genuinely special. Where else can you paddleboard with a 14th-century castle as your backdrop?

The river here is calm, peaceful, and perfect for beginners. Even if you've never been on a paddleboard before, this is an ideal place to learn. The water is generally flat, the current is gentle, and the scenery is spectacular.

We run paddleboarding sessions in Kent throughout the year on the River Rother. Sessions are suitable for complete beginners, families, and experienced paddlers looking for somewhere beautiful to explore.

The experience of gliding along the water, paddle in hand, with Bodiam Castle reflected in the river, is magical. It's peaceful, it's beautiful, and it's the sort of experience you'll remember for years.

Kayaking the River Rother

Kayaking on the River Rother in Bodiam is equally brilliant. The river meanders through stunning Kent and Sussex countryside, past the castle, through quiet farmland, and under ancient bridges.

The pace is entirely up to you. Want a gentle potter along calm water? Perfect. Fancy a longer expedition exploring further afield? Also brilliant. The Rother caters to all abilities and ambitions.

Wildlife is abundant here. Kingfishers flash past in brilliant blue streaks, herons stand motionless in the shallows, and if you're quiet and lucky, you might spot otters playing along the banks.

For families, kayaking here is fantastic. Children from around age 8 can paddle their own kayak, while younger kids can go in a double kayak with an adult. It's active, it's engaging, and it gets everyone away from screens and into nature.

Why the Rother is Perfect for Beginners

If you've never tried paddleboarding or kayaking before, the River Rother near Bodiam is one of the best places in South East England to learn.

The water is calm and sheltered. Unlike coastal locations where wind and waves can make things challenging, the river here is protected and peaceful. This means you can focus on learning technique rather than battling conditions.

The current is gentle. You're not fighting against fast-flowing water, which means less effort and more enjoyment. Even children and less fit adults can paddle comfortably.

The surroundings are stunning. Learning a new skill in beautiful surroundings just makes the whole experience better. Every direction you look, there's something lovely to see.

Walking and Hiking Around Bodiam

The countryside around Bodiam offers brilliant walking opportunities, from short strolls to longer hikes.

The 1066 Country Walk

This long-distance footpath passes right through Bodiam. You don't need to walk the whole thing (it's 31 miles from Pevensey to Rye), but the section around Bodiam is particularly scenic.

The walk takes you through farmland, along the River Rother, past the castle, and through quiet villages. It's well signposted and suitable for families with older children.

Circular Walks from Bodiam

Several circular walks start and finish in Bodiam village, making them perfect for a half-day outing.

The Bodiam to Ewhurst Green walk is about 5 miles and takes you through typical Kent countryside, past farms, through woodland, and along quiet lanes. Allow 2 to 3 hours at a comfortable pace.

The River Rother circular follows the river for much of its route, giving you lovely water views and the chance to watch wildlife. It's relatively flat and suitable for families.

Local pubs make perfect start or finish points. Nothing beats a country walk followed by lunch at a traditional Kent pub.

Walking with History

What makes walking around Bodiam particularly interesting is the history. This area saw significant action during the Norman Conquest and later conflicts. The landscape is dotted with historic buildings, from medieval churches to ancient farmhouses.

Information boards at various points explain the area's history. It's a brilliant way to combine exercise with learning, especially if you have children who think history is boring.

Cycling in the Bodiam Area

The quiet lanes around Bodiam are brilliant for cycling. Traffic is generally light, the countryside is beautiful, and there are plenty of interesting places to stop.

Family Cycling

For families with children, the roads around Bodiam offer safe cycling in stunning surroundings. The terrain is relatively gentle (this isn't hilly Kent), and there are several easy circular routes.

The lanes are narrow but quiet. You'll encounter the occasional farm vehicle, but proper through traffic is rare. It feels like proper countryside cycling.

Longer Routes for Keen Cyclists

More ambitious cyclists can explore further afield. The area offers excellent routes through the High Weald, with more challenging hills if you want a proper workout.

The back roads between Bodiam and Rye make a lovely route, taking you through Ewhurst Green and along quiet lanes with expansive views. It's about 8 miles each way, perfect for a half-day ride with a pub lunch in Rye.

The Bodiam and Wittersham Road and Kent and East Sussex Railway

Train enthusiasts (and children who love trains) shouldn't miss the Kent and East Sussex Railway. This heritage steam railway runs between Bodiam and Tenterden, with Bodiam station right by the castle.

The journey takes you through beautiful countryside, behind a vintage steam locomotive. It's nostalgic, it's fun, and it offers a completely different perspective on the area.

Combined tickets are available if you're visiting both the castle and taking the steam train. It makes a brilliant day out, especially for families.

Wildlife Watching Around Bodiam

The Bodiam area is excellent for wildlife watching. The mix of river, farmland, and woodland creates diverse habitats supporting abundant wildlife.

Birds

The River Rother is brilliant for birdwatching. Kingfishers are regularly spotted (that flash of electric blue is unmistakable), herons are common, and you might see cormorants, various ducks, and in summer, swallows and martins.

The surrounding farmland attracts buzzards, kestrels, and in winter, fieldfares and redwings. Bring binoculars if you're keen on birds.

River Wildlife

The Rother supports healthy populations of fish, which in turn attract otters. Sightings aren't guaranteed (otters are shy), but they're definitely here. Early morning or dusk offers the best chances.

Water voles live along the banks, though you need to be patient and quiet to spot them. Their tell-tale "plop" as they dive into the water is often the first sign they're around.

Family Activities in Bodiam

Bodiam is brilliant for families looking for things to do that everyone will enjoy.

What Children Love About Bodiam

The castle tops most children's lists. Exploring battlements, climbing spiral staircases, and pretending to defend against invaders never gets old.

Water activities are hugely popular. Children as young as 8 can paddle their own board or kayak, while younger children can go with adults. It's active, it's different, and it's genuinely fun.

The steam train appeals to children of all ages (and many adults). There's something magical about a steam locomotive.

Wildlife spotting engages curious children. Give them a mission (spot a kingfisher, find a heron, identify trees), and even reluctant walkers become enthusiastic explorers.

Making a Day of It

A typical family day in Bodiam might include:

  • Morning paddleboarding or kayaking session on the River Rother (2 hours)
  • Picnic lunch by the castle or at a local pub
  • Afternoon exploring Bodiam Castle (1 to 2 hours)
  • Optional steam train ride or walk (1 hour)

It's a full day, it's varied, and it's the sort of day out everyone remembers.

Local Food and Refreshments

After outdoor activities, you'll want refreshments. Bodiam has you covered.

The Bodiam Boating Station

Right by the river, this little cafe serves hot drinks, ice creams, and light snacks. It's perfect for a post-paddle refreshment, with outdoor seating overlooking the water.

Nearby Pubs

Several excellent pubs are within easy reach of Bodiam:

The Castle Inn at Bodiam is the obvious choice, right by the castle entrance. Traditional pub food, local ales, and a welcoming atmosphere.

The Queen's Head at Icklesham (about 15 minutes drive) is a proper country pub with excellent food and a great beer garden.

The White Dog Inn at Ewhurst Green offers good food and a friendly welcome in a traditional setting.

All three welcome families and understand that after a day outdoors, people are hungry.

Best Times to Visit Bodiam

Bodiam is brilliant year-round, but different seasons offer different experiences.

Spring (March to May) sees the countryside burst into life. Flowers bloom, lambs appear in fields, and the weather is generally pleasant without being too hot. Paddleboarding and kayaking are comfortable with a wetsuit.

Summer (June to August) is peak season. The castle is busiest, but everywhere is at its best. Long days mean you can pack in more activities. Water activities are at their best, often possible in just shorts and a T-shirt.

Autumn (September to November) offers spectacular colours as leaves change. It's quieter than summer but still warm enough for outdoor activities. The autumn light makes the castle look particularly spectacular.

Winter (December to February) is quietest. The castle looks magical in frost or snow, and crisp winter days are perfect for walking. Water activities are still possible with proper wetsuits, and the peace and solitude are special.

Getting to Bodiam

Bodiam is in East Sussex, right on the border with Kent. It's easily accessible from London and towns across the South East.

By Car

From London, take the A21 towards Hastings, then the A229 and B2244 to Bodiam. Journey time is around 1.5 to 2 hours depending on traffic.

From Tunbridge Wells, it's about 30 minutes via the A21 and local roads.

From Hastings, head north on the A21, then follow signs to Bodiam. About 20 minutes.

Parking is available at the castle (National Trust members free, otherwise small charge) and at the boating station.

By Public Transport

Bodiam has a railway station on the Kent and East Sussex Railway (heritage steam line). For mainline trains, the nearest stations are Robertsbridge (4 miles) or Battle (7 miles), from where you'll need a taxi or bus.

Combining Bodiam with Other Local Attractions

Bodiam makes an excellent base for exploring the wider area.

Rye is just 8 miles away. This beautiful medieval town has cobbled streets, antique shops, and excellent restaurants. It's worth at least a half-day visit.

Battle and the Battle of Hastings site is about 10 miles away. The 1066 battlefield and abbey are fascinating for history enthusiasts.

Camber Sands offers completely different scenery. These massive sandy beaches are about 15 miles from Bodiam, perfect if you want a beach day after river activities.

The High Weald surrounds Bodiam, offering endless opportunities for walking, cycling, and exploring picturesque villages.
